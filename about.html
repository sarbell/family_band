About

The Cox and Palkki families both have a multi-generational heritage of faith and music, which they celebrate and strive to cultivate.

Our Story

Our families, the Cox and Palkki's, met in 2012 while participating in a summer music camp. After our initial connection, we found we had much in common, including our passion and love for creating and sharing music.

In the fall of 2013, the Cox and Palkki Family Band was created. Since that time, we have enjoyed performing at countless venues for various audiences throughout Northern Utah. We feel a deep sense of purpose and joy as we celebrate our heritage of faith, family, and music together.

Our Families

The band members from the Cox and Palkki families range from ages 12 to 75. Both groups came from families who valued making music as a family. The kids grew up teaching eachother how to play different instruments, and experimenting with making their own arrangements to songs that they liked. We often has guests joining us, including grandparents and friends. Each member plays at least one instrument and also enjoy singing together.

Meet the Band Members

Some members of the band have been making music since they were 2 years old! Some have a bachelor's degree in music, and some are self-taught musicians who just enjoy making music. With close to 20 members (and growing!), our band is full of varying backgrounds, and personalities, which makes our music especially unique. Meet the people who make the magic happen:

dan & Nancy

Banjo - trumpet - trombone - guitar

Dan and Nancy are Kristi's parents and seasoned music lovers. Dan is a life-long musician. He plays the trombone, trumpet, and really anything you can think of, but primarily plays banjo in the band. Nancy has been the ultimate 'groupie' since the band first started. She is our biggest suporter!

Kristi & Matt

Piano - band managers

Kristi and Matt the parents of the Palkki side of the group. They are the people that make the show run! From providing instruments, transporting drum sets and hosting rehearsals at their home, they are the backbone of the group. Kristi and Matt both play the piano and sing.

Emilie & Doug

Piano - band managers - Saxophone

Emilie and Doug are the parents of the Cox side of the group. They are key supporters for the band and inject energy into the group. Emilie plays the piano (and has a Bachelor's in piano performance) and Doug primarily plays the saxaphone, but you may see him sneak in some guitar during some performances.

Grant & Gabrielle

Vocals - Violin - Keyboard - Melodica - Mandolin

Gabrielle, Grant and Gary make up the Gibbons family. Gabrielle and Grant both have a Bachelor's in music and have grown up playing and performing various instruments since a young age. They are the parents of Gary, who loves to wiggle and clap to the music they play.

Kylee & Tyler

Violin - Vocals - Mandolin - Saxophone

Kylee and Tyler make up the Payne family. Kylee is one of our main vocalist and violinist. She is also a skilled guitar and mandolin player. Tyler is also a musician and adds some jazz to the group with his saxophone skills. They both love music and sharing the gifts they have with others.

Max & Sarah

Guitar - Vocals - Bass

Max has been a musician from when he was a little boy. He loves to play the upright bass, electric bass and guitar. He loves performing and sharing music with those around him in jazz bands, orchestras, combos, and any other venue he can play at. Sarah can kind of play the kazoo, but mostly just supports the band.

Chaz & Shaleene

Electric Bass - Guitar - Vocals

Chaz and Shaleene were recently married, making Shaleene the newest member of the band. Chaz provides the core groove of the group on his electric bass, and through his larger than life personality. Shaleene also plays the guitar and has been a huge support to the group since they started dating.

Romney

Drums - Violin - Accordion

Romney started playing music on the violin, but now is an avid drummer. He not only has great drum rolls, but he'll make you roll over laughing. He is currently a student at Weber State University studying Health Administration. He also participates in multiple jazz groups at the university.

Zach

Guitar - Vocals

Zach has played and performed music with his family since a young age. He performs the guitar and also provides vocals for the group. He is currently going to school to be a nurse at Weber State University. In addition to performing music, Zach enjoys fishing, food, and being active.

Grant

Bass - Cello

Grant primarily plays the electric bass and cello in the family band. In addition to music, Grant has interests in the field of robotics and engineering. He has a strong sense of creativity and has built many custom bat-a-rangs and other creations built in his dad's workshop.

MaKell

Vocals - Violin - Ukulele - Banjo - Mandolin

MaKell is skilled in a wide variety of instruments. She performs primarily on violin, but also joins her grandpa on banjo on many arrangements. MaKell is currently studying cosmetology. She is the life of the party and also performs along side Gabrielle and Kylee in their vocal trio.

McKay

Vocals - Guitar

McKay has an unquenchable passion for learning. Whether he is playing guitar, or reading a new book, he is always eager to share what he has learned. He has a contagious laugh and smile that elevates every performance he is a part of. McKay is an avid Star Wars fan, Among Us player, and also loves the outdoors.

Nate

Drums - Guitar

Nate is another one of the bands fabulous drummers. Nate brings a serious beat, and a great smile to the group that makes playing together even more fun. Nate also plays the guitar and loves to go kyaking and camping with his family. He also enjoys fishing and playing lots of fun games.

TJ

Percussion - Guitar - Vocals - Cello

TJ, or Talmage, is one of the youngest players and brings a tremendous amount of energy and talent to the group. He plays the guitar, cello, train whistle, cowbell, and any other percussion instruments. TJ is an avid lover of having fun and will always make your day with the stories he has to tell.
